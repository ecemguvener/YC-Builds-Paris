The Sharp Problem

Hackathon teams build products, then have zero customers to prove anyone cares.

The infrastructure problem underneath

Agents cannot safely represent a startup unless they have a separate identity: their own inbox, number, calendar, permissions, and action history. Borrowing a founder's accounts is unsafe. Generic automation is forgettable. A named teammate with receipts is memorable.

The Winning Demo

Use one concrete vertical: a team built an AI receptionist for restaurants. The app creates Maya, a customer-discovery agent, and turns the idea into outward motion.

Paste the startup idea

"We built an AI receptionist that answers restaurant reservation calls."

Spawn the agent identity

Maya gets an email address, phone identity, booking link, outreach permissions, and a kill switch.

Generate ICP and leads

The agent identifies small restaurants with high phone volume and produces a curated prospect list.

Ask for founder approval

The founder reviews the message and approves one external action.

Act and leave receipts

Send a real email, show a simulated call transcript, create a booking link, and log every permission check.

End on the trophy

The dashboard says: 10 leads found, 3 contacted, 1 interview booked.
